================================================================================
INFORMATION ABOUT THE DOCUMENT
================================================================================

This guidebook is the source of truth for all facts about the supermarket environment. It is the only document given as a knowledge base. 
Each entry is written as complete, self-contained manner so that a retrieved chunk contains all the information needed to answer the question 
without requiring context from surrounding chunks. Entries repeat key facts (such as a zone's location) so any single chunk can stand on its 
own when retrieved.

Canonical IDs:
- ZONE_XX  : named zones and sections of the store
- AISLE_XX : walkable corridors between shelf rows or between a shelf and a wall
- FEAT_XX  : special features e.g. a cart, supply cart, barriers, etc.
- ITEM_XX  : products with fixed locations
- ROUTE_XX : navigation instructions between two locations

DIRECTIONAL CONVENTION

All "left" and "right" references are given from the customer's point of view, where the default view is facing south into the store after entering 
through the main entrance. This means:

- "Right" (from customer view) = west (compass direction)
- "Left" (from customer view) = east (compass direction)
- "Ahead" or "in front" (from customer view) = south
- "Behind" (from customer view) = north (towards the entrance)

For example, when the document says "the produce section is on the right when entering", this means it is in the north-WEST corner, to the customer's right
because the customer is facing south.

Compass directions (north, south, east, west) are absolute and refer to walls. Compass directions are given from a birds eye view of the supermarket:
- North wall: the wall containing the entrance.
- South wall: the wall containing Fridge 2 and Fridge 3.
- East wall: the wall containing the TV screen.
- West wall: the wall in front of which Fridge 1 sits.

Compass directions are used to avoid ambiguity in directions.

SECTION 0: SUPERMARKET ENVIRONMENT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The supermarket is a single open room. The entrance is in the centre of the north wall. When entering, customers face south into the store (customer's point of view). 
The west side of the store contains the fresh produce displays in the north-west corner and the main shelving area below them, made up of four vertical shelf rows.
The east side of the store contains the cashier desk in the north-east corner, the bakery shelves below the barriers (which sit just south of the entrance
for crowd control, to avoid direct random access to the store), and the east shelving area of three horizontal shelf rows. The south wall and south-west corner house 
three fridges and two freezers.
The "Fresh Food" decorated wall runs along the west half (customer's right) of the north wall (indicating where the fresh food section is), ending at the entrance. The right
half of the north wall is plain. A TV screen is mounted on the east wall in the north-east corner, in front of the cashier's desk.

Floor plan, birds-eye view (north = top, entrance = north-centre):

- North wall west half (customer's right): "Fresh Food" decorated wall.
- North wall east half (customer's left): plain wall, with a TV at the far east end mounted on the east wall.
- Entrance: centre of north wall, with a basket pile to its right and a shopping cart bay to its left from the customer's view.
- North-east corner: the cashier desk (exit only, customers leave through here after paying and cannot be used as entrance to the store).
- North-west corner: fresh section shelving (two display tables with fruit and vegetables).
- Centre-west: the main shelving area, with four vertical shelf rows (Shelf 1, Shelf 2, Shelf 3, Shelf 4) running north to south. Shelf 1 is westernmost whilst 
  Shelf 4 is easternmost.
- Centre-north: barrier queue dividers, immediately south of the entrance. These barriers block direct southward passage from the entrance; customers must enter 
  the main store through the fresh produce section (ZONE_02). Below the barriers are the two bakery shelves, which sit to the west of the cashier desk.
- Centre-east: the east shelving area (ZONE_09), containing three horizontal shelf rows (Shelf A, Shelf B, Shelf C) stacked from north to south. Shelf A is northernmost whilst Shelf C is southernmost.
- South-west corner: Fridge 1 (the dairy fridge), against the west wall. It ends before AISLE_01 begins, meaning no overlap between Fridge 1 and the west wall corridor.
- South-centre: Freezer 1 and Freezer 2, side by side. Freezer 1 is to the west of Freezer 2 and viceversa.
- South wall: Fridge 2 and Fridge 3, side by side, to the east of Fridge 1, along the south wall. Fridge 2 is to the west of Fridge 3.
- West wall corridor (AISLE_01): a narrow corridor between Shelf 1 and the west wall, with a re-supply cart parked near the north end.
- East side: open floor space east of the east shelving area, leading to the east wall where the TV is mounted.

Customer flow: when entering, customers must go right and through the fresh produce section, and can navigate through open spaces to the cashier desk, 
which leads to the exit (same door as entrance).

The supermarket is a single-room indoor store designed as a research environment for a virtual human guide. The store contains all the categories
listed in Sections 1, 2, and 6: fresh produce, bakery, dairy, three fridges, two freezers, and seven shelf rows of packaged goods, household items, and
pet supplies.

The virtual human guide of the supermarket is known as the VH. The VH exists to help customers find products and locations within the supermarket. The VH
does not physically move through the store; the VH answers questions verbally about where items are and how to navigate to them. The VH knows
everything contained in this guidebook and nothing else.

8.1 Similar-Name Disambiguation

Fresh vegetables versus frozen vegetables: Fresh vegetables are sold in the Fresh Produce Section (ZONE_02), on the open display tables in the
north-west corner near the entrance. Frozen vegetables are sold in Freezer 1 (ZONE_06) at the south-centre of the store. These are two different
locations. If a customer asks for vegetables without specifying, ask whether they want fresh or frozen.

Fresh fruit versus frozen fruit: Fresh fruit is in the Fresh Produce Section (ZONE_02). Frozen fruit is in Freezer 1 (ZONE_06). If a customer asks for
fruit without specifying, ask whether they want fresh or frozen.

Fresh tomatoes versus canned tomatoes: Fresh tomatoes are in the Fresh Produce Section (ZONE_02). Canned tomatoes are on Shelf 3 (ZONE_05). The
store does not supply frozen tomatoes specifically. If a customer asks for tomatoes, clarify which form they want.

Bottled juice (cold) versus bottled juice (room temperature) versus fresh juice: This store does not sell freshly squeezed juice. Bottled juice is
available in two forms: cold bottled juice in Fridge 2 (ZONE_07), and room-temperature bottled juice on Shelf 1 (ZONE_05). If a customer asks for
juice, clarify whether they want cold or room-temperature.

Cold drinks versus room-temperature drinks: Cold drinks (water, soft drinks, juice, beer, iced tea) are in Fridge 2 (ZONE_07) along the south
wall. Room-temperature drinks (water, juice cartons, soft drinks) are on Shelf 1 (ZONE_05) in the main shelving area. If a customer asks for drinks
without specifying, clarify which type they want.

Cold ready meals versus frozen ready meals: Cold (non-frozen) ready meals are in Fridge 3 (ZONE_07) along the south wall. Frozen ready meals
are in Freezer 2 (ZONE_06). If a customer asks for ready meals without specifying, ask whether they want cold or frozen.

Bread (fresh) versus packaged bread: All bread in this store is fresh and is in the Bakery Section (ZONE_04). The store does not supply packaged or
sliced bread on the main shelves. If a customer asks for bread, the answer is always the bakery shelves.

Fish and meat: This store does not have a fresh fish or fresh meat counter. All fish and meat is frozen and located in Freezer 2 (ZONE_06). Sliced cold
cuts (ham, salami) are in Fridge 3 (ZONE_07) — these are processed cured meats, not fresh meat. If a customer asks for meat, clarify whether they
want frozen meat (Freezer 2) or sliced cold cuts (Fridge 3).

Fridge 1 versus Fridge 2 versus Fridge 3: The store has three upright fridges. Fridge 1 (ZONE_03, the Dairy Fridge) is in the south-west corner
against the west wall. Fridge 2 (ZONE_07) contains cold drinks and is along the south wall. Fridge 3 (ZONE_07) contains ready meals and deli
products and is along the south wall, to the east of Fridge 2. These are three distinct fridges with different contents and positions.

Freezer 1 versus Freezer 2: The store has two chest freezers. Freezer 1 is to the west and contains frozen vegetables, frozen fruit, and frozen pizza.
Freezer 2 is to the east of Freezer 1 and contains ice cream, frozen ready meals, and frozen fish and meat.

Freezers versus fridges: The store has two chest freezers (Freezer 1 and Freezer 2) and three upright fridges (Fridge 1, Fridge 2, Fridge 3). The
freezers contain frozen food. The fridges contain refrigerated but non-frozen products. If a customer asks for "the freezer" they mean the
chest freezers (ZONE_06). If they ask for "the fridge" without specifying, ask which.

Bakery shelves versus bakery aisle: This store does not have a dedicated bakery aisle. The bakery section consists of two shelves in the centre-north
area below the barriers (ZONE_04). All these names refer to the same place.

Shelf 1 versus Shelf A: The store has shelves with both numeric labels (1 to 4 in the main shelving area) and letter labels (A to C in the east
shelving area). Shelf 1 is the westernmost vertical shelf in the main shelving area (ZONE_05) and holds beverages. Shelf A is the northernmost
horizontal shelf in the east shelving area (ZONE_09) and holds household and cleaning products. These are different shelves in different parts of the
store.

Vertical shelves versus horizontal shelves: Shelves 1 to 4 in the Main Shelving Area (ZONE_05) are oriented north-to-south (called "vertical" in
this guidebook). Shelves A to C in the East Shelving Area (ZONE_09) are oriented east-to-west (called "horizontal" in this guidebook). All seven
shelf rows are of normal supermarket height.

Customer-view "left" versus compass "east": Throughout this guidebook, "left" and "right" are from the customer's view facing south after entering.
The customer's right corresponds to compass west, and the customer's left corresponds to compass east. So the produce section, which is "on the right
when entering", is in the north-WEST corner.

the only way out of the store after paying. A customer cannot exit through the entrance, and cannot enter through the cashier.

SCENARIO_01 — Bread, milk, and eggs

Items requested: bread, milk, eggs. Route summary: Bread is in the Bakery Section (ZONE_04). Milk and eggs are
both in the Dairy Fridge (ZONE_03, Fridge 1). A good route is: from the entrance, turn right into the produce section, then walk south through
AISLE_01 (the west wall corridor) to Fridge 1 in the south-west corner for milk and eggs, walk east along the bottom, then north through the 
open floor between the shelving areas, to reach the bakery in the centre-north, then exit via cashier.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO_02 — Pasta, pasta sauce, and cheese

Items requested: pasta, pasta sauce, cheese. Route summary: Pasta is on Shelf 2 (ZONE_05). Pasta sauce in jars is on
Shelf 3 (ZONE_05), the next shelf to the east. Cheese is in the Dairy Fridge (ZONE_03, Fridge 1). A good route is: from the entrance, turn right
into the produce section, then enter AISLE_03 from the north. Pick up pasta from Shelf 2 on your west side and pasta sauce from Shelf 3 on your east
side. Continue south, then turn west to reach Fridge 1 in the south-west corner.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO_03 — Snacks and drinks for a party

Items requested: chips, chocolate, soft drinks, beer. Route summary: Chips and chocolate are on Shelf 4 (ZONE_05).
Room-temperature soft drinks are on Shelf 1 (ZONE_05). Beer is in Fridge 2 (ZONE_07). A good route is: from the entrance, turn right into the produce
section, walk south through AISLE_02 (drinks on Shelf 1 to the west of the aisle), then through AISLE_04 to reach Shelf 4 (snacks on the east side of
the aisle), then continue south to Fridge 2 along the south wall for beer.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO_04 — A fresh meal: vegetables, frozen fish, rice

Items requested: fresh vegetables, frozen fish, rice. Route summary: Fresh vegetables are in the Produce Section (ZONE_02), on
the right when entering. Frozen fish is in Freezer 2 (ZONE_06). Rice is on Shelf 2 (ZONE_05). A good route is: pick up vegetables in the produce
section on entry, walk south through AISLE_02 or AISLE_03 to pick up rice from Shelf 2, continue south to Freezer 2 in the south-centre.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO_05 — Cleaning supplies and toiletries

Items requested: laundry detergent, toilet paper, shampoo, toothpaste. Route summary: Detergent and toilet paper are on Shelf A (ZONE_09). Shampoo
and toothpaste are on Shelf B (ZONE_09). A good route is: from the entrance, turn right into the produce section, walk south through any aisle
of the main shelving area, then walk east past Shelf 4 to the east shelving area. Pick up Shelf A items, then move to AISLE_05 or AISLE_06 to access
Shelf B.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO_06 — Pet supplies and food shopping

Items requested: dry dog food, fresh vegetables, dairy. Route summary: Dog food is on Shelf C (ZONE_09). Fresh vegetables are in
the Produce Section (ZONE_02). Dairy is in Fridge 1 (ZONE_03). A good route is: pick up fresh vegetables in the produce section on entry, walk south
through AISLE_01 to Fridge 1 for dairy, then walk east through AISLE_07 (the south aisle) to access Shelf C for pet food.
